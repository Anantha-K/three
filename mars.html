MARS

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance, which comes from iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It’s a rocky planet with mountains, valleys, and even the largest volcano in the solar system, called Olympus Mons. Mars is known for its thin atmosphere and is a favorite for scientists and space explorers who dream of sending humans there one day!

Red Wonder: Mars is often called the “Red Planet,” but did you know it’s also sometimes called the “Desert Planet”? It’s like a giant rusty playground with lots of cool features!
Dusty Dancer: Mars experiences massive dust storms that can cover the entire planet! It’s like Mars throwing a giant party and getting all dusty—everyone’s invited!
Two Tiny Moons: Mars has two small moons named Phobos and Deimos. They look more like space potatoes than actual moons! Imagine roasting those for a cosmic snack!
Chilly Temperatures: Mars is really cold! Temperatures can drop to -80°F (-62°C) at night. If you were on Mars, you’d definitely want to wear your warmest pajamas!
